About me

I am a PhD scholar specializing in Knowledge-Driven Deep Learning and Medical AI. My current work focuses on anomaly detection in medical imaging, specifically developing clinically grounded AI architectures for the classification of Neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's Disease (AD) using T1w sMRIs.

I have a strong background in computer vision and deep learning architectures. Currently, I am exploring the intersection of Foundation Models and Vision-Language Models (VLMs) using Transformers to create interpretable and data-efficient solutions for healthcare. My research blends domain priors, transformers, and interpretable evidence to build robust models under real-world constraints (low data, shift, and reliability needs).
